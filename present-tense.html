Lesson of the Day: Present Tense

Samples
English Italian
I hear a bird. Io sento un uccello.
You pet the dog. Tu accarezzi il cane.
Luca eats an apple. Luca mangia una mela.
Isabella buys a shirt. Isabella compra una camicia.
You (all) plant the flowers. Voi piantate i fiori.
We follow the cat. Noi seguiamo il gatto.
They play outside. Loro giocano fuori.

Recipe

The Italian sentence structure is similar to English: Subject + Verb (conjugated) + Rest (direct object, preposition, etc.)

The hardest and most important part is learning to conjugate verbs according to their subjects. In English, an example would be I play vs he/she plays. In Italian, there are six conjugations per tense:
English Italian
I Io
You Tu
He/She Lui/Lei
We Noi
Y'all Voi
They Loro

Every verb has an infinitive which we start with to conjugate and they end in -are, -ere, or -ire These endings are replaced according to the subject. The present tense conjugations can be found in the table below.

Presesnt Tense Conjugations
-ARE -ERE -IRE
Cucinare Scrivere Aprire
Io Cucino Scrivo Apro
Tu Cucini Scrivi Apri
Lui/Lei Cucina Scrive Apre
Noi Cuciniamo Scriviamo Apriamo
Voi Cucinate Scrivete Aprite
Loro Cucinano Scrivono Aprono

Practice
English Italian.
I cook the pasta Io (cucinare) ____ la pasta.
The dog eats an apple. Il cane (mangiare) ____ una mela.
We sleep at midnight. Noi (dormire) ____ a mezzanotte.
You buy an ice cream. Tu (comprare) ____ un gelato.
Y'all write a book. Voi (scrivere) ____ un libro.
They live in Italy. Loro (vivere) ____ in Italia.
